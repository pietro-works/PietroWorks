Two layers make the glow

The accent phrase in the hero is not one gradient text. It is the same text twice: a crisp layer clipped to the spectrum in front, and a blurred duplicate breathing behind it. Remove the duplicate and the word goes flat. Blur the original instead and the word itself goes fuzzy. The two-layer build is the whole trick.

gradient 105deg·5 stops· bloom blur 9 to 16px·pulse 3.4s· opacity .18 to .68
#39e8ff · 0% #8cf7ff · 24% #00ff99 · 52% #a855f7 · 78% #39e8ff · 100%
note / 01

The duplicate is free

No second element in the markup. The em carries its own copy in a data-text attribute, and the ::after pseudo-element reads it back with content: attr(data-text). One node in the DOM, two layers on screen.

note / 02

Alone, the bloom is almost nothing

At rest it sits at 18% opacity behind the text. You can barely see it in the exploded view above, and that is the point. It is not a light source, it is the difference between a printed word and a lit one.

note / 03

The ramp loops back to cyan

The token gradient is a three-stop ramp, but the hero adds two stops: a pale #8cf7ff at 24% to lift the first word, and a return to cyan at 100% so the phrase ends as cool as it began. Long phrases get a full spectrum pass without drowning in purple.

note / 04

The breath is the only loop

The bloom pulses between 18% and 68% opacity over 3.4 seconds while the crisp layer never moves. The brand allows infinite loops only on small ambient signals, and this is one of exactly two in the hero.
