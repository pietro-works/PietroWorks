stage_03 · highlight

The system assembles around the person

In the "How I work" section, the portrait never moves. Everything else does. Three signal chips fly in and boot up, an orchestration meter charges, and then the whole apparatus bursts away and rebuilds. The argument of the entire site, played as a 9.6 second loop of pure CSS. No JavaScript touches this scene.

cycle 9.6s·3 signals · staggered 12 / 21 / 30%· quint-out arrivals·meter sweep cyan→green· 0 lines of JS
note / 01

Signals boot like hardware

Watch the dot on each chip as it lands: it flickers red four times on a step-end clock, then settles green. Status is earned on screen, not assumed. It is the smallest storytelling beat on the site and the one people remember.

note / 02

One clock, many instruments

Every element animates against the same --origin-cycle: 9.6s custom property. Arrival order, the meter's charge and the shared burst all read one variable, so retiming the whole scene is a one-line edit.

note / 03

The exit is choreography, not a cut

From 74% each chip leans out along its own vector, then bursts away from the portrait, scaling down as it fades. Every element carries --out and --burst coordinates, so the scatter radiates from the center like a released field.

note / 04

A layer built, then retired

The markup and CSS for a constellation SVG still exist in the source, three lines meant to draw between the signal chips. They ship with display: none on the live site and stay off here too: a leftover from the animation's build process, not a feature. The three chips carried the connective idea on their own, so the drawn links were cut without a redesign.

note / 06

Why the portrait holds still

The section claims the system runs the loop while the person keeps the judgment. The animation states it literally: tools arrive, link, charge and leave, and the human at the center never moves.
