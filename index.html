Blueprint of pietro.works

One page, taken apart. The site behind this repo is a single-page portfolio built like the private tools it describes: near-black navy glass, one luminous spectrum, monospace readouts, and motion that only moves when it has something to say. Below, every section is read closely and the signature constructions stand isolated on their own stages.

The voice

Quiet authority, engineered

The design never raises its voice because it does not need to. Three principles resolve almost every decision on the page.

principle / 01

Human judgment first

Every animation on the site ends the same way: a person makes the call. Dashboards keep a human-review step lit, the review queue waits for an approval stamp, and the orbit leaves the portrait untouched at the center. The technology draws itself around the decision, never over it.

principle / 02

Engineered, not decorated

Nothing on the page is ornament. The dot matrix is a texture with a mask, the ambient blobs are two radial gradients, and every label that reads like machine output is machine-plausible: file names with sizes, percentages with baselines, statuses that earn their color.

principle / 03

One glow per view

The accent spectrum is a spotlight, not wallpaper. Each viewport gets a single luminous moment: the hero phrase, the live dot, the meter at full charge. When two things glow, nothing glows, so the page spends its light carefully.

The fingerprint

Details that fakes always miss

Surface copies of this identity fail on the same six details. They are small, byte-exact, and they carry most of the signature.

glow

The accent is two layers

Crisp gradient text in front, a blurred duplicate breathing behind it. Single-layer gradient text is the most common fake and always reads flat. Stage 01 explodes the construction.

line-s · cyan 22%
line · cyan 10%

Hairlines are tinted, never gray

Every border on the site is cyan at 10% or 22% opacity. A neutral gray 1px border is the fastest way to break the instrument-panel read.

◇ validation-loop.js 4.2 kb

Metadata speaks mono

Indices, eyebrows, timestamps and file names are DM Mono. Sentences are DM Sans. The contrast between the two is the "this person ships code" signal.

Depth is colored

Shadows tint toward navy, glows use the accent tokens, and the one green dot always carries its 0 0 9px halo. A generic black drop shadow reads instantly off-brand.

One easing curve

cubic-bezier(.22, 1, .36, 1) everywhere. Motion spends its speed early and decelerates with confidence. Never linear, never springy.

Radii are large and specific

32, 22, 14, 8. Pills are 999. There are no 4px web-default corners on big surfaces anywhere on the page.

Section by section

A close read of the page

Eleven bands, one idea each. Every section states a claim and then demonstrates it, usually with motion doing the arguing.

01

Navigation

sticky pill · hairline

A single floating pill: avatar, four anchors, one gradient call to action. It stays a pill at every width because the site has one job and the nav should not suggest otherwise.

02

Hero

pointer tilt · count-up

The claim leads from the left while a mock operations dashboard runs on the right, counting processed work and cycling its pipeline. The whole window tilts a few degrees toward the pointer. Enough to feel alive, never enough to become a toy.

03

Metrics band

spotlight · late reveal

Four proof cards that trade numbers for mechanisms: hours become minutes, the person keeps the call, no training deck, modules over scripts. The band reveals only after it fully clears the viewport edge, a deliberate beat later than everything else.

04

How I work

9.6s cycle · pure css

The portrait holds still while context, handoff and verified output orbit it on a shared 9.6 second clock. The scene is the thesis of the site drawn as choreography. Stage 03 takes it apart.

05

Capabilities

stagger reveal · stroke icons

Eight cards, one 1.5px stroke-icon system, at most two tags each. Outcomes lead and tools follow, because a keyword wall would undo the calm the rest of the page works for.

06

Architecture

split demo · 7.5s loop

An NDA banner concedes the best work cannot be shown, then proves the shape of it anyway: a five-node flow diagram and an animated review queue that plays manual chaos against the automated lane. On the left, a blocked cursor mistypes and stalls. On the right, four stages ship and an approval stamp lands.

07

Process

icon wake · 330ms steps

Four numbered steps from watching the human workflow to shipping a real product. The icons wake in sequence as the row enters view, and the copy insists on the part most portfolios skip: mapping before automating.

08

Reliability

ghost cursor · build loop

A shelf of six module files, an empty canvas, and a synthetic cursor that builds the system in front of you, wires it, acknowledges it and clears it. The section claims modularity and then performs it. Stage 04 isolates the full choreography.

09

Toolkit

3d flip · 720ms

Five flip cards that serve two readers: named tools on the front for the person who evaluates stacks, plain outcomes on the back for the person who signs off. Stage 05 isolates the first card and its turn.

10

Experience

accent rail · reveal

Three blocks stand in for a timeline, each one a category of shipped work rather than a parade of job titles. Most of the work lives under NDA and the section says so plainly instead of gesturing at logos.

11

Contact

canvas particles · orbit rings

The portrait returns, this time inside a canvas of up to 118 orbiting particles and two elliptical accent rings, drawn per frame and paused the moment the section leaves the viewport. The ask stays one line: email or LinkedIn, skip the sales deck.
